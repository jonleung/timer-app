Since Notion doesn't allow direct Vercel embeds, we can use CodePen (which IS whitelisted) as a bridge!

Step 1: Go to CodePen

Create a new Pen on CodePen:

Open CodePen â†’

Step 2: Add this HTML

In the HTML section, paste this:

<iframe 
  src="https://timer-app-notion.vercel.app" 
  style="width: 100%; height: 100vh; border: none;">
</iframe>

Step 3: Save your Pen

Click "Save" in CodePen (you might need to create a free account)

Step 4: Get the Embed URL

After saving, your URL will look like:
https://codepen.io/[username]/pen/[pen-id]

Step 5: Embed in Notion

In Notion:

  1. Type /codepen or /embed
  2. Paste your CodePen URL
  3. Click "Create embed"
Alternative: Use Replit
Replit is also whitelisted by Notion. You can:
  1. Go to replit.com
  2. Create a new HTML/CSS/JS repl
  3. Add an iframe pointing to your Vercel app
  4. Run it and share the repl URL in Notion
Why this works:
Notion uses Iframely which has a whitelist of ~1900 approved domains. CodePen, Replit, JSFiddle, and CodeSandbox are on this list, but custom Vercel deployments are not. By using these services as a "bridge", we can embed any content in Notion!
